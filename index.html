Science Bowl

The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) National Science Bowl is a nationwide academic competition testing knowledge in all areas of science and mathematics, including biology, chemistry, earth science, physics, energy, and math. Teams, comprised of four students and one alternate, face-off in a fast-paced question-and-answer format, and Paly SciBowl has two or more teams. HB is the teacher who serves as an advisor and coach.

DOE created the National Science Bowl (NSB) in 1991 to encourage students to excel in mathematics and science and to pursue careers in these fields. More than 344,000 students have participated in the NSB throughout its 34-year history, and it is one of the nationâ€™s largest science competitions.

Each year, we compete in one of the 65 high school regional Science Bowl tournaments at SLAC lab. The next step is NSB finals competition, which is hosted in Washington D.C. every May.
