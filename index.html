National Science Bowl ® (NSB)

The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) National Science Bowl is a nationwide academic competition testing knowledge in all areas of science and mathematics, including biology, chemistry, earth science, physics, energy, and math. Teams, comprised of four students and one alternate, face-off in a fast-paced question-and-answer format, and Paly SciBowl has two or more teams. HB is the teacher who serves as an advisor and coach.

DOE created the National Science Bowl (NSB) in 1991 to encourage students to excel in mathematics and science and to pursue careers in these fields. More than 344,000 students have participated in the NSB throughout its 34-year history, and it is one of the nation’s largest science competitions.

Each year, we compete in one of the 65 high school regional Science Bowl tournaments at SLAC lab. The next step is NSB finals, which is hosted in Washington D.C. every May.

Our Mission

Paly SciBowl is committed to increasing access to STEM opportunities through preparation for competitions and building a tight-knit community of aspiring scientists and mathmaticians. We are proud to be student led and we believe that working together to suceed in SciBowl inspires our collective passion for STEM.
